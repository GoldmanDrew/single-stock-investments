## What this business is

Applied Digital builds and operates high-power data centers for AI and HPC workloads. **[Fact]** Tenants pay long-term lease rent for energized megawatts; Applied Digital does not own the GPUs. Revenue comes from colocation and hosting fees as campuses come online, not from selling compute cycles directly. **[Fact]** FQ3 2026 (Feb 28, 2026) revenue was $126.6 million (+139% YoY) with $44.1 million adjusted EBITDA excluding the held-for-sale Cloud Services segment. **[Fact]** The company has signed leases for 600+ MW across Polaris Forge 1, Polaris Forge 2, and Delta Forge 1, funded partly by secured notes including $2.15 billion for Polaris Forge 2 post-quarter. **[Fact]** GAAP net loss to common was $(100.9) million in the quarter; equity is subordinate to a layered secured debt and preferred stack.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market prices Applied Digital as a speculative AI infrastructure story with binary execution risk, not as a contracted lease book approaching cash-flow breakeven. **[Inference]** GAAP losses, stock-based compensation ($48.9 million in FQ3), and CoreWeave concentration push generalists away before adjusted economics scale with energization. **[Inference]** If 600+ MW energizes on disclosed timelines, forward owner cash flow per share could exceed what a loss-making GAAP multiple implies. There is no clean equity yield curve with one dated payoff; return depends on a campus energization stack over several years.

#### Return math in plain English

At **$46.85**, base case assumes **$1.25** forward owner cash flow per share growing 22% for five years then 10%, exiting at 16x in year ten. That compounds to roughly **11.5%** per year. Bear case (5%/3% growth, 10x exit) yields **-3.7%**; bull (30%/12%, 22x) reaches **19.3%**. The starting $1.25 is **[Inference]** from FQ3 annualized adjusted EBITDA path (~$0.63/sh) plus contracted MW ramp, not GAAP EPS.

**Upside / downside from price:** Bull **19.3%** p.a. if all campuses energize on time; bear **-3.7%** on delay/default. Downside is **not** bounded by book: secured debt absorbs asset value first. Hohn screen (~30% upside / ~10% downside): **fail** on both counts at current capital structure.

**Returns statement:** We expect **~11.5%** per year over ten years at **$46.85** if contracted MW energizes on schedule; primary risk: **refinancing or construction delay** on stacked secured debt before owner cash flow scales.
