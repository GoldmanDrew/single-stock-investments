## What this business is

Intercontinental Exchange operates global exchange, clearing, and market-data infrastructure plus mortgage-technology workflows. **[Fact]** Customers pay transaction, clearing, listing, and subscription fees across three segments: Exchanges, Fixed Income and Data, and Mortgage Technology. Revenue scales with trading and clearing activity, not balance-sheet lending. **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenues were $9.9 billion with adjusted free cash flow of $4.19 billion; Q1 2026 net revenues hit a record $3.0 billion (+20% YoY). **[Fact]** The exchange segment runs at roughly 80% adjusted operating margin. CEO Jeffrey Sprecher has led the company since its founding.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

There is no dated payoff or structural discount here. **[Inference]** The stock pulled back from a ~$189 high while Q1 2026 showed energy revenue +46% and financials volume +65%, so the market may be treating peak transaction activity as permanent. **[Inference]** Our base return uses mid-cycle FY2025 FCF per share, not the Q1 run rate. Return is franchise earnings power at price, not a mispricing signal with a known catalyst date.

## Executive summary

Intercontinental Exchange is a toll collector on global market activity: exchanges, clearing, data, and mortgage rails with minimal principal risk. Twenty consecutive record revenue years and ~80% exchange margins confirm franchise quality. At ~$153, Lawrence base case implies ~11.0% annualized return over ten years on $7.41 mid-cycle FCF per share, below the 15% hold band. A separate Hohn EPS bridge ($6.95 toward ~$8.50 in three years at 22× exit) implies ~18% if volume momentum persists, but that reconciles with cyclical starting FCF, not a permanent step-up. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) stays partial because transaction revenue is cyclical. We propose **watch** and would revisit near $115–125 for ~15% base IRR.
