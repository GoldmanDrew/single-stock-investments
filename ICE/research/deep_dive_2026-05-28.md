## What this business is

Intercontinental Exchange operates global exchange, clearing, and market-data infrastructure plus mortgage-technology workflows. **[Fact]** Customers pay transaction, clearing, listing, and subscription fees across three segments: Exchanges, Fixed Income and Data, and Mortgage Technology. Revenue scales with trading and clearing activity, not balance-sheet lending. **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenues were **$9,931M** with operating income **$4,929M** and net income **$3,315M** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-K_20260205_rpt20251231_acc0001571949_26_000004.htm`). **[Fact]** Q1 2026 net revenues were **$2,977M** (+20% YoY vs **$2,473M**); net income **$1,413M** vs **$797M** (`investor-documents/sec-edgar/10-Q_20260430_rpt20260331_acc0001571949_26_000007.htm`). CEO Jeffrey Sprecher has led the company since its founding.

## Executive summary

Intercontinental Exchange is a toll collector on global market activity: exchanges, clearing, data, and mortgage rails with minimal principal risk. FY2025 filings confirm **$9,931M** net revenues, **$4,929M** operating income, and **$28,915M** stockholders' equity; Q1 2026 accelerated to **$2,977M** net revenues and **$1,413M** net income on strong Exchanges volume. At **$153**, Lawrence base case implies **~11.0%** annualized return over ten years on **$7.41** mid-cycle FCF per share, below the 15% hold band. A separate Hohn EPS bridge still implies higher near-term returns if volume momentum persists, but that reconciles with cyclical starting FCF, not a permanent step-up. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) stays **partial** because transaction revenue is cyclical. Leverage (~**$19.6B** total debt per FY2025 10-K) adds integration risk from Black Knight. We propose **watch** and would revisit near **$115–125** for ~15% base IRR.
