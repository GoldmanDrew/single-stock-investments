## What this business is

OTC Markets Group operates regulated US over-the-counter equity markets (OTCQX, OTCQB, OTCID, Pink) and FINRA-member ATS infrastructure (OTC Link, extended-hours trading). **[Fact]** Issuers pay listing and disclosure subscription fees; brokers pay ATS and market-data fees. Revenue is subscription- and market-data-heavy with limited principal risk on core operations. **[Fact]** FY2025 net revenues were $107.9 million; diluted EPS was $2.58 (+14% YoY). Q1 2026 net revenues were $33.8 million (+15% YoY) with diluted EPS $0.59 (+18%). Roughly 11.86 million diluted shares outstanding.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

No dated payoff or structural discount. **[Inference]** Niche croupiers rarely re-rate to 15% IRR while growing; at ~$52 the stock trades near ~22× FY25 earnings. **[Inference]** Headline net revenue growth understates core fee growth when transaction pass-through grosses up revenue and expense lines. Return is franchise earnings at price unless regulatory or volume scares create a cyclical entry.

## Executive summary

OTC Markets is US OTC listing and ATS infrastructure: subscription-heavy fees on disclosure tiers (including new OTCID tier) and extended-hours trading. Q1 2026 accelerated (+15% net revenue YoY) with capital-light economics. At ~$52.11, Lawrence base case implies ~9.7% annualized return over ten years on ~$2.35 mid-cycle owner cash per share, below the 15% hold band. Bull case ~14.6% if OTCID and international listings compound faster. Asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is partial: low capex and recurring mix help, but regulatory and volume tails exist. We propose **watch**; illustrative entry ~$42–45 needed for ~15% base IRR.

Entry **~$42–45** illustrative for ~**15%** base IRR. Hohn screen: **fail** at spot; downside **partial** (regulatory/volume tails).
