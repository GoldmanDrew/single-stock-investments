## What this business is

San Juan Basin Royalty Trust holds a roughly 75% net-overriding royalty interest in Hilcorp-operated natural gas properties in northwestern New Mexico. **[Fact]** The Trust has no employees and no control over drilling or costs; Argent Trust Company administers distributions. **[Fact]** FY2025 royalty income from Hilcorp was **$0**; unit-holder distributions have been **none** since May 2024. **[Fact]** Excess production costs of $6.19 million gross ($4.64 million net to the Trust) must be recovered from future gross proceeds before royalties resume. **[Fact]** Cash reserves were $14,380 at March 31, 2026; the trustee draws on a line of credit for expenses. **[Fact]** May 18, 2026: no monthly distribution declared.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

The market may treat San Juan as a broken income trust heading for wind-down, missing the Horizon Kinetics equity yield curve lens on a transitory NPI deficit. **[Inference]** Excess production costs fell from ~$15.9M net (Dec 2024) to ~$6.3M net (Dec 2025), roughly $9.6M recovered in one year, but Hilcorp's 2025 capex ($23.8M actual vs $9.0M plan) slowed the path versus HK's May/June 2025 reinstatement estimate. **[Inference]** Royalty trusts sit outside yield screens and index mandates, creating a market-structure discount. If distributions resume, cumulative payoff may exceed what a zero-yield price embeds; timing and going-concern risk are worse than HK assumed.

---

## Executive summary

San Juan Basin Royalty Trust is a passive gas NPI royalty with zero distributions since May 2024 while Hilcorp recovers excess production costs. Net deficit fell ~60% in 2025 but cash is nearly exhausted and going-concern doubt is disclosed. At ~$4.15 (Yahoo, May 26, 2026), base equity-yield-curve return is 5.8% over eight years ($6.50 payoff); bull 19.4% in six years if gas spikes and costs clear fast. Dhando is none today: zero cash flow, depleting reserves, line-of-credit dependence. We propose watch: optionality only if unit price discounts recoverable NPI rather than terminal wind-down.
