## What this business is

Teqnion AB (TEQ.ST, Nasdaq First North) is a Swedish industrial serial acquirer. **[Fact]** The parent owns 30+ decentralized niche-industrial subsidiaries; revenue is earned in local markets (components, specialty manufacturing, services), and the Stockholm-listed company compounds via bolt-on M&A and operating improvements, not via a single product line. **[Fact]** FY2025 net sales grew with acquisitions while reported organic net sales were **-5%** after deliberate exits of unprofitable units. **[Fact]** FY2025 free cash flow ex-acquisitions was **173.1 MSEK**; Q1 2026 EBITA margin hit a record **14.3%**. Cash returns to shareholders are modest; the model is reinvestment plus EPS growth.

---

## Why the market might be wrong

Reported **organic net sales -5%** may anchor sentiment while margin and cash-flow inflection are under-discounted. **[Inference]** Q1 2026 EBITA margin **14.3%** (from **8.1%** a year earlier) and FX-neutral organic **+7%** excluding five worst contributors suggest pruning is working. There is no dated HK payoff; mispricing, if any, is franchise quality at a full multiple (~**15.7×** FY2025 FCF/share at **~158 SEK**).

## Executive summary

Teqnion is a proven decentralized serial acquirer with a strengthening margin and FCF story: FY2025 EBITA +36%, Q1 2026 EBITA margin **14.3%**, and FCF ex-acquisitions **173.1 MSEK**. Organic sales remain negative on headline numbers because management exited weak units; that is discipline, not decay. At **~158 SEK** (First North, ~May 22, 2026), base expected annual return at today's price (Lawrence) is **13.7%** on **10.08 SEK** FY2025 FCF/share, below the **15%** accumulate band. Durable competitive advantage (Munger moat) is stable at the portfolio level; asymmetric payoff (Pabrai dhando) is partial with M&A risk. We propose **watch** unless price falls toward **~130 SEK** for a **~15%** base IRR.
