# AI Disruption Lens

Apply when evaluating business durability, moat, and capital allocation.

## Questions
1. **Automation exposure** — Can core workflows be automated or commoditized by AI?
2. **Data advantage** — Does the company own proprietary data that improves with scale?
3. **Distribution** — Is customer access defensible or easily disintermediated?
4. **Cost structure** — Does AI reduce their costs faster than it reduces barriers to entry?
5. **Product vs wrapper** — Is value in the product itself or in orchestration that models could replicate?

## Scoring (qualitative)
- **Resilient** — Proprietary data, regulated workflows, physical assets, or network effects AI cannot easily replicate
- **Mixed** — Some durable advantages, some automatable middle-office or support functions
- **Vulnerable** — Business model is primarily information arbitrage, content aggregation, or low-switching-cost services

## Use in reports
Note AI-relevant risks and opportunities explicitly. Do not treat AI as a blanket negative or positive — tie claims to specific business lines and cite primary docs.
