================================================================================
A. INVESTMENT PHILOSOPHY (screening layer)
================================================================================

"Absolute rather than relative value" — minimum 25-30%+ upside, ~10% expected
downside on longs. Target ~15% p.a. long-term return irrespective of market direction.

"Our investments have high barriers to entry — operating generally in monopolies or
oligopolies... investing first and foremost on the basis of a strong valuation case and
business franchise." (TCI Letter 2004 Q1)

Longs: high and growing earnings/FCF yield; dividend often above short rates.
Shorts: 30-50%+ clear downside WITH catalyst; stand-alone worth vs market price
(e.g. HypoVereinsbank €11-14 vs €21.5, rights issue catalyst). (2004 Q1)

Beta ~0.35–0.43 on long book — low market sensitivity by design.

================================================================================
B. POSITION WRITEUP STRUCTURE (repeat every deep dive)
================================================================================

Hohn's quarterly "Portfolio Review" follows a consistent anatomy:

1. QUARTER CONTEXT
   - Position size (% of fund), QTD/YTD price move, P&L contribution
   - What happened in the quarter (results, news, management change)

2. OPERATING TRENDS (numbers, not narrative)
   - Latest quarter vs prior quarter vs consensus
   - Volume/activity metrics with trough-to-recovery when cyclical
   - Pricing, mix, margins — explicit % changes
   - Separate one-offs: provisions, rebates, integration, FX

3. THESIS PILLARS (2-4 structural drivers, each quantified)
   Examples from letters:
   - Union Pacific: (a) legacy contract re-pricing over 5 years, (b) ops gap vs peers
   - Deutsche Börse: cost program €50m+€50m = 15% EPS growth even flat revenue
   - Visa: cash-plastic substitution runway + pricing as value share of system revenues
   - Charter: penetration 45%→55%, capex step-down 2019-20, mobile option $20-35bn

4. FIELDWORK
   - Management meetings (UP Omaha, Airbus CEO, India bank trip)
   - Investor days (Visa SF, Lockheed F-35 Texas)
   - Where sell-side is wrong ("underestimate operational rebound potential")

5. COMPETITIVE / DISRUPTION
   - Structural vs cyclical label on every driver
   - Disintermediation watch (Visa mobile/online; Coke vertical integration)
   - Political/regulatory itemized +/- (Wellpoint reform bill)

6. VALUATION BRIDGE (always explicit)
   Methods used across letters:
   - Forward P/E or earnings yield (Coke 6.3% yield vs 5% 10yr avg)
   - FCF yield + growth = total return (Oracle 8% + 5-7% = ~14%)
   - EPS path × exit multiple → IRR (Charter $40 FCF × 15x = $600, 27% 3yr)
   - FCF yield vs bond (J-Power 15% vs 1.5% JGB)
   - NAV / RAB / cap rate (Aena 5% normalized LFCF yield; RAB 1.1x)
   - Liquidation / holdco discount (Altaba 24% vs pre-tax NAV)
   - "Embedded value for free" (Lockheed F-35 uplift in multiple)
   - Normalized vs pro forma (DB synergies, Lloyds provisioning peak)

7. RETURNS STATEMENT
   Always closes with expected % return and horizon:
   - "15-20% long-term" (rails, exchanges)
   - "12% IRR" / "15% 3-year IRR" (Alphabet after price move)
   - Upside/downside range on event paths (Fox 10% base, 25-35% if Comcast returns)

8. PRIMARY RISK (one dominant)
   - UP: double-dip recession
   - Near-term macro vs long-term structure (CSX 2007)

================================================================================
C. ANALYTICAL TECHNIQUES (tools to replicate)
================================================================================

OPERATING LEVERAGE
- Incremental margins on volume recovery (rails, exchanges)
- Cost/income ratio moves from synergy programs (Lloyds -4% to 44%)

REVERSIONARY PRICING
- Legacy contracts rolling off on known schedule
- 25% of contracts re-priced upward (TCI on rails)

NORMALIZATION
- Cycle peak/trough on volumes before applying multiples
- Pro forma for M&A synergies before comparing to peers

HIDDEN VALUE
- Subsidiary at zero in sum-of-parts (utility inside miner)
- Option at zero (Waymo) vs peer comp (GM Cruise $12bn)

CAPITAL STRUCTURE
- Balance sheet capacity → distributable cash (Aena €11bn = 50% mkt cap)
- Equity vs credit same thesis (Lloyds CoCo 11% YTM vs equity recovery)

TAM SIZING (with skepticism)
- Visa $3tn processed vs $11tn "addressable" — note padding risk when TAM broad

ACTIVISM / GOVERNANCE (when relevant)
- Public letters, board accountability, fiduciary conflicts (Fox/Disney/Comcast)
- ROE/ROA decline + cross-shareholding critique (J-Power)

PEER BENCHMARK
- Cost per unit vs named peers
- Pricing vs footprint competitor (Charter vs Comcast broadband)

D. EXAMPLE PASSAGES (for citation)
================================================================================

Deutsche Börse valuation + cost bridge (2007 Q4):
"We estimate that cost reduction alone, even if the overall top line were flat in 2008,
would give at least a 15% earnings growth... We see a more realistic scenario medium-term
of double digit earnings growth without capital giving a 15% per annum medium-term return."

Union Pacific pillars (2007 Q4):
"First, major reversion of pricing from large legacy contracts... over the next five years.
Second, UP's management is focused on operational improvement... to close the gap between
UP and its peers."

Oracle valuation case (2010 Q1):
"The valuation case is that the stock trades on x 13 our 2011 earnings estimate...
around an 8pc free cash yield that can grow around 5-7pc giving around a 14pc medium
term total return."

Charter return bridge (2018 Q2):
"We forecast $40 free cash flow per share in 2021. Based on a 15x exit multiple we think
the shares can get to $600 in 3 years, an annualised rate of return of 27%."

Entry criteria (2004 Q1):
"Our longs are absolute rather than relative value in stocks where we can see a minimum
25-30%+ upside and expected 10% downside risk."
