The key points of our exposures are as follows: 
   
(1) The Fund did not move into a fully invested position until March. As in any new 
fund, performance was negatively impacted by holding cash balances whilst 
completing this process. 
 
(2) The Fund was invested at quarter end 158% gross (longs plus shorts/NAV) and 
122% net long with almost no outright short exposure at quarter end. The share 
class arbitrage positions represent non-voting shares which have offsetting longs 
of the same company’s voting shares.  We view these as low value at risk 
investments. 
 
(3) The Fund was invested at quarter end 117% in outright longs (and net long) in 
Europe and 23% in outright longs (and net long) in Asia. The Asian exposure is 
almost completely in South Korean stocks. The Fund is 33% net long in Italy. 
 
(4) Exposures are substantively in defensive, lowly rated stocks including utilities, 
tobacco companies, toll roads, stock exchanges, food stocks, retail banks, and 
incumbent telephone companies. 
 

 
(5) Exposures are highly concentrated. Top 5 holdings (excluding share class 
arbitrage) represent 65% of the Fund’s NAV.

Longs 
Our longs are absolute rather than relative value in stocks where we can see a minimum 
25-30%+ upside and expected 10% downside risk. On average, our stocks are trading on 
a high (frequently greater than 10% of market capitalization) and growing earnings and 
free cash flow yield in a European environment which currently has 2% short term 
interest rates.  Most of our stocks have dividend yields significantly in excess of short 
term interest rates and many match or exceed that of long interest rates at 4.1%. 
 

 
We believe that our investments have high barriers to entry to their businesses – 
operating generally in monopolies or oligopolies.  We do not feel these are relative values 
which depend on the market for their performance in the long term.  In several cases there 
are catalysts for stock performance, but in all cases we are investing first and foremost on 
the basis of a strong valuation case and business franchise. 
 
For those investors which have signed our confidentiality agreement, our top long 
positions are detailed in the Appendix to this letter. 
 
Shorts 
On the short side we are finding few stocks in Europe which meet our minimum criteria 
of 30-50%+ clear downside with catalysts.  One of the major reasons for this is that we 
think that European markets are not expensively valued. Morgan Stanley estimates that 
the European market trades on around 13 x their estimated earnings on 2004 versus 18 x 
estimated earnings for the S & P 500.  Nonetheless, we do not feel that we have yet been 
as effective on the short side as we can be but have been disciplined to only put on short 
exposure where we feel we have a margin of safety. 
 
One example of a meaningful short position taken during the quarter (it was a 2.5% short 
for us) was Bayerische Hypovereinsbank in Germany. We went short this stock at 21.5 
euros on the basis that a) we estimated its stand alone worth at €11-14 (based on a 
discount to pro forma book value of 17 due to structurally poor returns on equity and 
poor asset quality) and b) we had a catalyst. Despite aggressive asset sales during the last 
year, including Hypo Real Estate and Bank Austria, it was still short capital necessary in 
order to maintain its credit rating. In March, they announced a rights issue for 3 billion 
euros which, unlike most other recent European rights issues, caused the stock to fall to 
16 euros where we covered our short. 
 
For those investors concerned about our lack of short exposure it is worthwhile reminding 
you that our strategy is not to short for the purpose of market hedging or volatility 
smoothing because our time horizon is long term and not short term   This is particularly 
the case because (a) we think our stocks are lowly rated absolute values and (b) (partly as 
a result of (a)) our stocks have a low market sensitivity measured by their Beta.   We see 
shorts as a separate strategy in their own right rather than as a hedge to our longs or as a 
market hedge. Nonetheless it is worth noting that the Fund has no protection against the 
systemic risk of a market collapse.   
 
Asian Exposure 
As we highlighted to investors during our marketing, we are a global Fund and will invest 
outside of Europe if we find compelling values.  Long term we feel, like many others, 
that Asia offers superior economic fundamentals to Europe and the US.  Within Asia we 
have identified Korea as a very cheap stock market, especially for mid and small cap 
stocks, and have invested approximately 22% of the portfolio there. We started investing 
in our biggest holding in Korea at a P/E of under 3 x and a 75% discount to our estimate 
of intrinsic value.  
 
For risk management purposes we have decided to limit our fund exposures to any single 
country outside of Europe and the United States to a maximum of 20-25% of the Fund.   
As a result, we are considering the idea of offering a small Asian overflow fund for a 
limited number of investors for ideas in Asia which hit our portfolio limit on a country or 

 
stock specific basis due to risk and liquidity reasons but which we feel offer compelling 
value. We are happy to discuss this with any interested investors.

Foreign Currency Exposure 
The Fund has and will continue to always hedge its European currency exposures into 
dollars for dollar class investors.  However, we have currently decided not to hedge our 
Korean Won exposure.  The rationale for this is that our largest investment there has 
significant export earnings which will be negatively impacted by Won appreciation.  
Moreover, the overall market, for the same reason, is likely to also be hurt by a 
significant Won appreciation.  The currency is managed by the South Korean 
Government against appreciation adding weight to our concern on this risk of currency 
appreciation.  So in summary, not hedging the currency in this case acts as a natural 
(albeit imperfect) hedge to our stock positions, except in the case of devastating geo-
political events. The Won strengthened by 4% in the quarter and we estimate that this 
currency effect added c.70bp to portfolio returns.

Leverage 
We think that our current portfolio’s low beta makes it appropriate to use a modest 
amount of leverage in the Fund.  However, we are currently continuing to find attractive 
opportunities and may consider accepting a small amount of additional capital in order to 
manage this leverage to an appropriate level of risk.  We will discuss this issue on our 
conference call with all of you, but want to note that we do not wish to ever dilute returns 
through carrying excess capital since the partners of the firm and the Foundation are 
amongst the largest investors in the Fund. 
 
Stock Market Summary 
For the first two months of the year equity markets continued their strong rally of 2003.  
However, the terrorism in Madrid served as a catalyst for a market correction in March 
eliminating all the gains for the year in both the US and Europe. Asian markets, however, 
have appreciated 10-15% during the first quarter reflecting continued strong economic 
fundamentals and fund inflows into these markets.  Ten Year Euro swap rates have 
declined in Europe from 4.3% 6 months ago to nearer 4.1% today as record short term 
rates stay on hold and, according to recent ECB comments, may move lower. 
 
Economic Climate 
The economic picture in Europe is weak with Q4 GDP rising a meagre 0.4%. The strong 
euro has clearly exacerbated the structural weaknesses of European economies. In this 
context we feel comfortable being in defensive companies until we see more value in 
economically sensitive businesses. 
 
Staffing 
Our analyst and operations team have generally been settling in and performing well. 
Unfortunately, due to personal reasons, Mads Gensmann has decided to resign shortly 
after joining us. We are sorry to have lost him since he was doing well with us. We are 
currently in discussions with one experienced candidate as a replacement.  We are also  
undertaking a search process for an analyst hire to be based in Asia. 
 
The Children’s Investment Fund Foundation 
Total estimated foundation contributions earned (both paid and accrued) in the first 
quarter were $723,000. We hope that investors will value the social bottom line that we 
expect the foundation will create. The Foundation will give a short mid year update of its 
work in the next quarterly investor letter. 
 
Summary 
We feel that we have solid investment ideas in a difficult investing environment. We are 
very focussed on understanding our companies as well as we can. For nearly all our 
portfolio companies in both Europe and Asia, we have met with top management in 2004 
to discuss their businesses in depth and understand their strategy.
