Portfolio Update 
 
Deutsche Boerse  
Deutsche Boerse has three similarly sized businesses, all with strong monopolistic 
characteristics: 
(1) the German stock exchange  
(2) Eurex – one of the world’s largest derivatives exchanges  
(3) Clearstream – a clearing and settlement business primarily for bonds.    
 
At our average cost, the company had a market cap of approximately €5bn with €700m of 
net cash.  It had been a small position (1-2% of NAV) for us during 2004.  The more we 
analysed the company, however, the more we realised that the valuation, at a 11-12% free 
cash flow yield to EV (for structurally growing businesses with both high barriers to 
entry and ROEs), was half intrinsic value.  The main reason for this was concern over 
value destructive acquisitions, lack of understanding by the market of the quality of the 
businesses, and little appreciation of the opportunity to leverage the balance sheet which 
had 15% of the market cap in net cash. 
 
In December 2004 the company announced a proposal to buy the London Stock 
Exchange at a price which we estimated gave at best a 5% return on investment, leading 
to value destruction of 5-10% of the intrinsic value. However, regardless of whether the 
deal went through, we saw 50-100% upside in the stock, with the high end if the deal 
could be stopped and the balance sheet leveraged.  We accumulated a holding of just 
under 8% of the company by the end of Q1 2005 (representing 20% of the fund’s NAV) 
and once we reached 5% filed in the shares and formally requested an EGM to remove 
the entire supervisory board.  This was our only option to stop the deal since, under

During Q1 shareholders representing, we believe, over 50% of the shares privately and 
publicly asked the company to stop the LSE deal and in March were successful in 
persuading the management to terminate the proposed deal and initiate a large share 
buyback program of 7% of the shares and probably double this amount within a year.  We 
support these buybacks as being value accretive at current share prices.  The stock has so 
far appreciated approximately 25% over our average cost. 
 
It has been a valuable experience in the pluses and minuses of activist investing. At the 
end of the day we believe that companies are owned by shareholders and should be run 
for them.  When managements in our companies do things against shareholders’ interests, 
we will always work to protect our investors, as we promised you we would from the 
inception of our fund.  It has also been a good example of where having longer term 
capital has allowed us to create value in our investments which we would not have 
otherwise have been able to do. 
 
Euronext 
During Q1 Euronext also made a proposal to purchase the LSE but did not name a price.  
Its bid has been referred to the UK’s Competition Commission for up to 6 months.  
Euronext has significant synergies of combining with LSE but the price it can pay to still 
make a value accreting deal is in the range of £4 to £5 per share and not higher in our 
opinion.  We have made our views clear to the Euronext management and, in this case, 
any deal must be approved by shareholders who we believe are rational and share our 
opinion.  Harris Associates, the largest shareholder of Euronext (with 10% of the shares) 
has also publicly stated that £5 per share is at the limit of their view of value creation and 
they will oppose a value destructive offer.  During the quarter, Euronext’s stock price, 
which had been flat during 2004, appreciated 20% as investors re-rated the whole stock 
exchange sector in Europe (which has been trading at deep discounts to global peers for 
the last few years) and warmed to the option value of a potentially value accreting deal. 
 
LG Corp 
LG Corp, after having doubled during 2004, appreciated more than 40% in Q1 2005.  The 
stock started the year at a 60% discount to NAV and some 3X earnings for the second 
largest industrial conglomerate in Korea after Samsung Group. The catalyst for 
performance was the announcement by Sovereign Asset Management that they had 
bought $1bn of stock in LG Corp and LG Electronics (its primary subsidiary).  Sovereign 
is a private family investment fund with one of the very best records in investing in 
emerging markets.  They have waged a very high profile battle with SK Corp in Korea to 
improve its corporate governance in what has been a home run investment for them. 
 
During March, I visited the CFO of LG Corp in Seoul (as well as its key subsidiary 
companies) to review our investment.  I have been impressed by the professionalism with 
which the company has conducted itself during our investment and the implementation of 
a royalty plan that will probably create as much as $2 billion of value for LG Corp 
stockholders.

KT & G 
During my trip to Korea I met with the CEO of KT & G.  KT & G has announced plans 
to repurchase 24% of its shares 2004-2008.  Indeed, in 2004 nearly 8% were repurchased. 
The CEO assured me that the plans for stock repurchased and not cancelled (a maximum 
of 1-2% of the outstanding shares per annum) were to be used (a) in lieu of salary 
increases for employees if profit budgets were exceeded and (b) necessary social welfare 
contributions in order to maintain relations with the government.  2005 earnings will be 
depressed due to destocking after stockpiling by retailers ahead of a tax hike on January 1 
2005.   
 
On 2006 earnings we see the company trading on EV/Operating profit of around x 4.  A 
similarly sized market cap (circa $5bn) regional comp, HM Sampoerna, was bid during 
the quarter for cash by Altria at more than 3X the valuation of KT & G.  We are not 
expecting a takeover of the company, but we do believe the company could take itself 
private with its own balance sheet through share repurchases.  Templeton has filed with 
7% of the company (we own directly and in swaps around 6%).  We have asked the CEO 
to resume share buybacks aggressively in 2005 and are hopeful that this will occur.  The 
company is also paying a 5% dividend yield while we wait. 
 
European Toll Roads and Utilities 
Our European toll roads and utilities all appreciated during the quarter despite a sharp 
correction in many of them mid quarter on concerns over rising rates and rising bond 
yields.  As a reminder, our utilities are nearly all inflation-proofed by virtue of having 
their tariffs substantively indexed to inflation.  We value them with the assumption of 3% 
real rates versus the 2% implied by the European bond markets today.  We used the 
strength in stock prices to exit our large holding in Snam Retegas which had given us a 
total return of not far from 50% since the inception of the fund.  We have initiated a new 
6% position in a European utility with substantial upside to fair value that we prefer not 
to name at this point since we may further increase our holding and do not wish to 
prejudice this process. 
 
Indian Banks 
We have purchased a holding of over 6% of the Fund in a portfolio of 6 Indian banks.  
We believe that these will prove to be excellent investments trading on average at 6-9X 
normalised earnings, clean loan books, and with estimated earnings growth of 15-25% pa 
for many years into the future.  Indian financial services are nascent and the economy is 
booming.  The major short term risk to market valuations are rising interest rates (which 
is likely) but we feel that these risks are more than priced in to current valuations.  Both 
Patrick and I have twice been to India in the last 6 months to meet the managements of 
these banks.

despite EU pressure for a free market in M & A.  Banca Intesa is clearly a candidate in 
the medium term for M & A activity given the expressed desire of its largest shareholder,

TUI 
TUI’s annual report was released in Q1 and revealed another year of no underlying 
earnings in its core tourism business. Earnings were, for the third year in a row, created 
from provision releases and capital gains with no underlying cash generation.  Despite 
this the stock rallied 15% during the quarter increasing further our heavy cumulative 
losses on this short.    
 
Hyundai Mobis 
We have initiated a circa 5% position in this company whose business is supplying 
replacement and OEM parts to Hyundai and Kia cars globally.  It is a well run company 
with a very high ROE and good growth prospects trading at around 6-7X 2006 earnings.  
Interests are aligned with the Chairman of the group who personally owns some $500m 
of stock in the company, which is also the control vehicle for Hyundai Motor Corp.  I met 
with the CFO during my trip to Korea and was impressed by the pricing power which the 
company enjoys, the 75% market share of Hyundai/Kia in Korea and the correspondingly 
dominant share of Mobis in aftermarket parts.  As the installed base of Hyundai cars 
grows around the world due to the strong growth plans of Hyundai and Kia, Mobis will 
benefit. 
 
Emerging Markets Exposure 
We have been steadily increasing our investments outside of Europe over the last year, 
primarily in Korea and India.  We have also built positions in other Asian countries, 
Brazil and Russia.  In aggregate our emerging markets exposure (including Korea which 
some would argue no longer qualifies for this status) represents some 33% of NAV today. 
 
Our focus on Asia is being led by Oscar Veldhuijzen and supported by Damien Tran and 
John Ho. John joins us in May from Citadel where he ran a portfolio of US equities.  He 
is fluent in Mandarin and Cantonese and will be based in his native Hong Kong.  
Resumés for Oscar and John are attached in the appendix to this letter. 
 
During March, John, Oscar and I traveled to Hong Kong and Korea for two weeks of 
intensive company visits and broker meetings.  Our overall impressions were of dynamic 
economies and companies, but selective rather than broad based opportunities due to the 
strong run up in valuations over the last couple of years.  However, it is clear that 
substantial inefficiencies remain partly due to limited analyst disclosure and coverage.  
We feel that a dedicated deep value approach to these markets with local presence will be 
rewarded in the long term.  Since the Fund’s inception we have already made very 
substantial profits in Asia as evidence of this.

Assets Under Management 
The majority of the new capital taken in during January and the quarter was deployed in 
Deutsche Boerse and a European utility.  We had identified both opportunities prior to 
requesting the capital in line with our general policy of only taking capital when we have 
a specific need for it.  As discussed on the last investor conference call we have no 
precise total asset target.  If we do not feel comfortable that we can invest the capital that 
we have effectively and profitably we will return it to investors.  At this point in time we 
continue to believe that we have more good investment ideas than capital. 
 
New Horizon Fund 
We have recently agreed to take a minority stake of 25% in a new fund management 
company that will manage a dedicated Indian equity fund to be called New Horizon.  It 
will be managed by Madhav Bhatkuly, who for the last 6 years has been successfully 
managing a portfolio of small and mid cap Indian equities for Arisaig in India.  The new 
fund is expected to be launched in the next four to six months.  We believe that Madhav’s 
deep experience and contacts in the Indian market will help our Indian equity investing in 
the Master Fund and hence this project will benefit all our investors. It is not expected 
that the Master Fund will itself invest in New Horizon.

John Ho Resumé 
 
Prior to joining TCI, John was a senior equities analyst with Citadel Investment Group in 
Chicago, US. As part of Citadel’s event-driven group, John ran a fundamental equities 
portfolio with asset and capital restructuring characteristics.   
 
Before moving to the US, John was with the Boston Consulting Group (BCG) in Sydney, 
Australia, assisting Australian and New Zealand-based clients on corporate finance, 
value-based management and business strategy issues.  
 
Prior to BCG, John was lecturing finance and option pricing at the University of New 
South Wales, Sydney Australia, where he also graduated with First Class Honours and 
University Medal in finance and mathematics. His dissertation “Real Options Valuation 
of Australian Gold Mines and Mining Companies” was published in The Journal of 
Alternative Investments. 
 
John is a native Hong Kong Chinese, and spent time growing up and studying in Sydney, 
where he is married to another Hong Kong Chinese-Australian. John is also a 
professionally qualified piano and music teacher and performed in the Sydney Opera 
House.
