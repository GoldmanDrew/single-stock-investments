4. Portfolio Update 
 
Deutsche Boerse and Euronext 
 
Both companies continue to show extremely strong volume growth in both cash markets and 
derivatives, with average volume growth ranging from 30-40% year-on-year. 
 
On March 30th the board of Deutsche Boerse issued a press release supporting a merger with 
Euronext and proposing concrete negotiations. During March, Germany’s Chancellor Merkel 
and France’s President Chirac declared the potential merger to be ‘interesting.’ 
 
On April 3rd the TCI investment company through which we hold our Euronext stock 
formally requested a vote at the Euronext AGM in May on the principle of a merger with 
Deutsche Boerse. Euronext has formally agreed that this vote will take place. 
 
TCI continues to believe this merger would create significant value for shareholders and will 
try to ensure that managerial and political considerations do not stand in the way of 
shareholder wealth creation. 
 
Japanese Investments 
 
During Q1 2006, the Master Fund increased its investments in Japan. Partly because of the 
recent breaches of our confidentiality agreements we have decided not to disclose the names 
of these positions. In one case it is a deep value long idea of an outstandingly high quality 
infrastructure company that trades at 5x leveraged free cashflow (on a maintenance capex 
basis). We have a clear idea as to how its value can be translated into the correct share price 
but this may take 1-2 years to materialise. 
 
Our second significant position in Japan is a new holding this quarter. It is a hedged short 
idea which is currently 10% of NAV gross short and 5% of NAV net short. We are long 5% 
of NAV of a listed company which is the primary asset of this company as a hedge so that 
the short is effectively neutral to this asset. We consider the company to be a holding 
company trading at approximately a 40-50% premium to NAV, while it should be trading at 
a discount. This could be a volatile and loss making short position in the short-term but it is 
our belief that the fair value of the net short could easily be zero. The stock we are short is 
extremely liquid and we are short 1-2 days normal trading value.  As always, the purpose of 
the short is a stand alone profit centre rather than for portfolio hedging purposes. 
 
Altadis 
 
The Master Fund fully exited Altadis during the quarter after the Spanish government 
unexpectedly introduced a very low minimum tax and substantially increased its ad valorem 
taxes on the company, cutting our fair valuation of the company. 
 
Cemex 
 
The position in Cemex was increased during the quarter after a positive meeting with senior 
management. The position now represents just over 5% of NAV. We think the company

Chinese Estates 
 
During the quarter TCI was invited to take an 8% stake in the listed Hong Kong property 
company ‘Chinese Estates.’ Today the position represents 3% of NAV. We believe the assets

has been developing in Hong Kong for good corporate governance was a key reason for this 
invitation. We are working with management, who we think are excellent operators, to shrink 
the corporate governance discount. The stock has appreciated 30% since our investment.

9. Assets Under Management 
 
At the beginning of March we decided to fully redeem the one separate account the firm has 
been running since December 2003. For client confidentiality reasons, we will not disclose 
the identity of the separate account holder or the reasons for the redemption. The Master 
Fund purchased all the assets at market price. It should be noted that no special treatment was 
given to this client. Whilst $500m of new capital was taken to purchase the assets of this 
separate account, overall firm assets under management did not change. No net new capital 
has been employed since the end of November 2005. Today, with the redemption of the 
separate account and the return of the capital from the TCI Focus Fund, we only manage one 
fund – the TCI Master Fund. 
 
10. Confidentiality Agreements  
 
I would like to strongly remind all clients of the legally binding confidentiality agreements 
which they have signed. These agreements relate to the activities of our fund as well as our 
individual portfolio positions. We have been made aware of several breaches of these 
agreements recently. In one case our investor letter was emailed to a competitive hedge fund 
by a large family office. We have been informed by the client that the individual concerned 
will be fired. Clients should understand that all these actions hurt their own investments and 
breach their fiduciary duties. TCI will not hesitate to take appropriate action against any 
breaches of these agreements in the interests of all clients and the fund.
