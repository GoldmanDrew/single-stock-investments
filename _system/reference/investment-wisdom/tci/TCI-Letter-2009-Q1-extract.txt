3. Outlook 
 
Since the market low in early March we have seen a 25% rally in global equities.  The real 
economy fundamentals in the vast majority of cases have not improved.  Unemployment and 
underemployment are increasing everywhere at a fast pace, home prices are still declining in 
the US, and corporate profits are falling rapidly.  The debate that currently exists in the 
investment world is whether an economic recovery is being correctly anticipated by the 
market in advance of the actual recovery or whether this is another bear market rally.  Our 
view, from a bottom up perspective, is that we continue to see more equities that are fairly 
priced or over priced than are significantly underpriced.  Moreover, we think that in this 
environment of macroeconomic uncertainty we should demand a strong margin of safety in 
valuation to compensate for the risk that our profit projections turn out to be unachievable. 
 
We have added a limited number of new longs in Europe and the US and some shorts in 
Japan where we continue to see the worst economic situation of the major global economies.

6. CIFF 
 
As explained in our last investor letter, CIFF completed its move - via a redemption in 
specie - into a managed account vehicle on April 1st to enable CIFF to pursue a more 
diversified investment strategy, primarily focusing on fixed income.  The redemption in 
specie was carried out by transferring CIFF’s pro-rata share of each asset of the fund to the 
managed account. Where it was not possible to transfer a particular asset, arrangements 
have been put in place to ensure that CIFF remains economically exposed to such assets 
which will continue to be held by the fund. 
 
Given its investment objective, the opportunity set and the need for liquidity, CIFF may or 
may not participate in new long and short equity investments. At this point, CIFF’s portfolio 
has less than 10% of its assets in long equity positions. 
 
TCI has been appointed investment manager to the managed account and has appointed an 
external investment advisor in respect of the fixed income portion of CIFF’s investments. 
 
TCI has put in place an Allocation Policy that ensures that the respective investment 
mandates are adhered to and that investment decisions are effected fairly and in the best 
interests of both clients.   
 
7. Strategy 
 
At this point our strategy of investing long and short in equities globally remains 
unchanged. We do not rule out that we may invest in debt securities as permitted by the 
fund offering memoranda but we are concerned that the illiquidity of these securities may 
be at odds with investor desire for better liquidity. 
 
The portfolio is currently some 58% gross exposure in equities (longs plus shorts as a 
percentage of the fund NAV). We have been carefully making new investments in both longs 
and shorts during the quarter in stocks with good liquidity. Markets are extremely volatile at 
the moment and we expect the opportunity set for both longs and shorts to be equally 
volatile. Our committed capital allows us to take advantage of these opportunities.
