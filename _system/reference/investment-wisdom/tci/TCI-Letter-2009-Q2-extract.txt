3. Portfolio Review for Q2 2009 
 
Some of our core positions are outlined below in summary form. 
 
BAT 
 
We initiated a position in BAT in the quarter. BAT is the world’s second largest cigarette 
company with a leadership position in more than 50 markets worldwide. In common with 
other tobacco companies, BAT has strong defensive characteristics but additionally benefits 
from strong growth by virtue of generating  over 60% of operating profit in emerging 
markets. Unlike Western Europe and the USA, emerging markets continue to experience 
volume growth, which together with pricing should drive top line growth for the group of 3-
4%. Furthermore, BAT has announced a GBP 800m cost saving programme of which around 
50% should fall through to profits.  
 
A stable share price combined with underlying earnings growth further boosted by sterling 
weakness has seen the stock de-rate over the last couple of years from circa 13-14x earnings 
to 11x earnings and a 6% dividend yield. Company guidance is for EBIT to grow over the long 
term at around 6% but we believe this guidance largely excludes real price increases. Helped 
by significant sector consolidation over the last few years, we fully expect the recent positive 
pricing environment to continue which should enable BAT to exceed their operating profit 
guidance by several hundred basis points and increase bottom line by close to 10%. With no 
re-rating of the stock we would expect to generate a long-term high-teen shareholder return.  
 
E.ON 
 
Eon is one of Europe’s largest power generation utilities with significant regulated distribution 
assets and a strong gas pipe-line and supply business. We initiated the position subsequent 
to a profit-warning in March this year, since when the share price has rebounded 
approximately 25%. The value case for E.ON remains strong despite current tough trading 
conditions and the rebound in the share price. We believe the company can sustain €3.0 of 
earnings per share from 2010 putting it on 8x earnings whereas a fair multiple, in our 
opinion, would be closer to 11x.  Moreover, our earnings number is based on a long-term 
USD oil price in the mid 50s which, given that every USD10 increase in the oil price adds 10-
15% to fair value, provides us with additional optionality. 
 
Unilever 
 
Unilever is a leading consumer staples company with a portfolio of strong leading brands 
across a wide variety of categories including spreads, ice-cream, tea, deodorants and skin-
care.  In particular the business is distinguished by a very strong emerging market presence 
which generates almost 50% of operating profit and has delivered 7% hard-currency sales 
growth on average over more than a decade.   The company has been through a 10-year 
restructuring plan which has led to a degree of market fatigue but has finally resulted in a 
much leaner, stronger business.  Notwithstanding a tough current operating environment, we 
believe the new management team at Unilever is set to capitalise on this over the next few 
years by driving margins beyond 15% while invigorating top line growth to circa 5 % 
supported by 7-8% growth in emerging markets.  Unilever currently trades on an 8% levered

SAP 
 
SAP is the global leader in enterprise software, with a market share more than double that of 
Oracle, the number two player.  Demand for enterprise software is cyclical as evidenced by a 
34% reduction in new license sales in Q1 2009. Nevertheless, despite building in a 30% 
decline in software sales for the full year we believe SAP can keep its bottom line flat by 
virtue of a highly flexible cost base and a highly resilient maintenance and support business. 
We believe SAP has an effective lock over its maintenance business and will successfully 
implement a substantial price increase from 17% to 22% of the upfront software cost over 
the coming years which should help drive margins up from 25% to 35%.  Ignoring a cyclical 
recovery of new license revenues, we still believe SAP can deliver low double digit earnings 
growth beyond 2009 but the reality is likely to be much stronger as customers resume their 
software spending following major deferrals in the current year.  SAP currently trades on 15x 
2009 earnings compared with a historic rating in excess of 20x.  
 
Nestlé 
 
Nestlé is a leading global food and beverage company, with strong developed world category 
exposure and a sizeable emerging market business with dominant market shares and 
distribution assets built up over decades that are difficult to replicate. The product categories 
span powdered beverages (coffee, chocolate, milk), baby food, nutrition, pet food, 
confectionary, seasonings and frozen foods. In our view Nestlé is among the highest quality 
of the consumer staple names trading alongside some of the cheapest and on 13 x forward 
earnings, at a substantial discount to its average long term multiple of 16-17x. The company 
has delivered very strong long term performance, which we feel it is well placed to continue 
given the category and geographic exposures. 
 
Visa 
 
Visa is the world’s leading electronic payment network in a highly consolidated market with 
substantial brand, scale and economic barriers to entry. The economic model is among the 
most powerful and valuable we have come across, essentially a capex and cost free toll road 
on global consumer spending. The company takes a very minor share of system economics 
and we believe there is a material pricing opportunity going forward as the business moves to 
a for profit model, charging for services previously performed for free under customer 
ownership. In addition, the rise in plastic penetration continues unabated, both in the US and 
internationally, and this has provided a meaningful cushion to swings in underlying retail 
sales. 
 
During the quarter there has been persistent newsflow on actual and potential regulatory 
interventions in credit card practices and interchange (the fee paid by the merchant to the 
issuing bank). We believe both will accelerate an already existing trend away from credit 
cards to debit cards, but following precedents from other markets, will not materially impact 
the volume of transactions on cards overall. With respect to pricing we feel that both Visa and 
MasterCard continue to be a very minor share of system economics, below a natural level 
given their importance to the system, and that similar to other markets where interchange 
has been regulated, they are well placed to protect their economic model.  
 
On our materially above consensus numbers, the company is on 14x 2010 ex cash, which we 
consider extremely low for an asset and economic model of this quality.

Coke 
 
Coca-Cola is the leading non-alcoholic beverage company globally. The group has enormous 
post tax return on net tangible capital of close to 50%, which leads to very valuable organic 
growth. Around 80% of the group’s profits come from outside the US where the company has 
distribution assets 2-3x the scale of its nearest competitor. Further, around 50% of group 
profits come from emerging markets, where we feel the company is a very high quality 
exposure to developing world consumption growth.  
 
The US remains difficult for Coca-Cola and the innovation track record continues to be mostly 
poor. However with substantial scalability, ‘buy vs. build’ is highly accretive, Coke Zero has 
been a very successful launch, and while there are persistent structural and competitive 
issues in the US, the exposure at sub 20% is low, and the opportunity remains to improve 
profitability mid-term, potentially with the buy-out of the US bottling group.  
 
Coca-Cola is on 15x forward earnings at a material discount to the 20x historic multiple for 
the group, and with a clean balance sheet. 
 
Link Reit 
 
Link reported its full year recently which were impressive. Strong rental reversions continue, 
up 29% YoY vs. low 20s expected, with costs up only marginally driving double digit earnings 
growth. Dividends were up 13% YoY exceeding last year’s growth of 10% as rental reversions 
were even stronger than last year despite challenging economic conditions. Management 
continues to execute well and is increasing the scope of its asset enhancement program. 
Returns on refurbishments are better than guided with 4 out of 5 projects achieving a return 
on investment above 30%. The number of leases with Turnover Rent more than doubled, 
17% of all lease contracts and close to 30% of total revenues. In the next 2 years the asset 
enhancement projects completed will rise sharply and 45% of leases will expire which gives 
Link the opportunity to continue to grow their rental revenues. Gearing is still low at 24%, 
debt to total assets, and Link successfully refinanced HK$3bn of debt with the majority of its 
debt at fixed rate. Despite a strong stock price performance this year the stock is still on a 
5.5% dividend yield that can grow at double digits for some years. 
 
Bonds 
 
Where we can find liquid bonds in strong companies offering a strong equity return we may 
make investments in this asset class. At the moment approximately 6pc of the fund is 
invested in bonds. 
 
4. Outlook 
 
We remain sceptical that economies are really going to recover strongly and quickly.  
Unemployment in Europe and the US will move significantly past 10% and the credit cycle is 
still in its early stages.  Manufacturing and industrial companies continue to show limited 
signs of improvement in their businesses.  However, with interest rates at very low levels if 
we can find strong companies that can offer predictable 13-15% pa compounding this is 
attractive relative to interest rates at 3pc or lower.   We are actively seeking to increase our 
invested capital over the coming quarter.
