 TCI | Q2 2011 Report 
Portfolio Review for Q2 2011 
 
Lloyds Bank 
 
Lloyds hosted a strategic review presentation and provided long term guidance to the street on 
June 30th which boosted the stock price by over 10%.   
 
The new CEO, Antonia Horta-Osorio, presented his action plan which is focused on building a 
strong capital position, £1.5bn annual cost savings in 2014 and growth of the UK retail and SME 
businesses.  Lloyds announced an intention to significantly reduce non-core assets in Ireland, 
shipping, aerospace and to cut its international footprint in half.   
 
The company announced that it had conducted a detailed review of its provisioning and found it to 
be reasonable and adequate.  
 
Its guidance is for a core tier 1 capital ratio in excess of 10% from January 2013 when Basel III 
commences, which is in line with our estimates.  
 
Coal India 
 
In mid-July the Indian Prime Minister is expected to organise a meeting with all relevant ministries 
to attempt to find ways to reduce the serious coal shortages being suffered by India.   The Ministry 
of Environment and Forest (MoEF) has already lifted the moratorium for 9 out of 16 coalfields 
which will support Coal India‟s production growth in the next 1-3 years. 
 
A recently announced plan by a group of ministers to increase taxation on Coal India as part of 
new mining legislation has negatively impacted the share price.  We already model Coal India 
paying 13-15% of its net income in special taxes for local governments and local people.  We are 
skeptical that this current proportion will be materially increased. 
 
An announced symposium on coal washing to be attended by the various ministries and Coal India 
is very positive for our investment thesis that over time increased coal washing will lead to 
significantly higher coal prices for the company. 
 
Japan Tobacco 
 
Japan Tobacco (JT) recently announced that it will resume normal production mid-July instead of 
mid-August as previously guided.  
 
JT‟s response to restart production of all 73 of its products and substantially increase shipments 
from July 4th was well received by investors.  Furthermore, the purchase order ceiling for retailers 
will be released in August and JT will step up its marketing efforts to recover its market share as 
soon as possible.  
 
There have been talks recently to raise cigarette taxes to pay for the reconstruction of Japan.  This 
can easily be averted through an increase of JT‟s dividend payout, as explained on the TCI website 
www.jtchange.com.  The website has attracted thousands of visitors and has become an effective 
platform to exchange ideas to improve JT‟s capital management.  Two sell side brokers, as well as 
various newspapers and investors, have written about the Case for Change.  We believe that it will 
be very difficult for management and the board of JT to ignore the strong consensus which publicly 
opposes JT‟s poor capital management.   
 
BMW 
 
BMW reported strong volume growth of 16% for the month of June.  BMW branded cars grew 16%, 
MINI was up 34% and Rolls-Royce up over 60%.  Sales in China rose 41% in June and the 
company is also planning to raise prices in this region.

We continue to like the outlook for volume growth, mix and profitability for BMW and we think that 
the stock is still cheap despite its recent run-up. 
 
SAP 
 
Q2 was a busy quarter for SAP as it hosted Sapphire, its major event for customers, partners and 
investors. 
 
Overall, the feedback from Sapphire was positive − the favourable IT spending trends that we saw 
in 2010 have continued into 2011 and partners see their SAP business growing solidly this year.   
 
There was a huge amount of interest in SAP‟s new HANA (High performance Analytic Appliance) 
product at Sapphire.  HANA combines a powerful analytical engine with its proprietary in-memory 
database to allow real-time analysis of complicated data sets.  For example, airlines can use HANA 
to price tickets in real-time, rather than waiting for a batch IT process at the end of the day and 
large taxi companies could use the software to organise jobs as they come into the system.  SAP 
management are talking about revenue of $1m per box.  The returns from using this software are 
considerable and they are cautious about under-pricing it. 
 
As well as having numerous meetings at Sapphire, we also visited SAP at its Walldorf headquarters 
to meet the co-CEO, Jim Snabe and the FD, Werner Brandt.  We were impressed with the HANA 
and mobile growth initiatives and also its focus on driving up margins which will be achieved 
through operational gearing − “we don‟t need any more developers than we have today” was one 
of the comments from the CEO to us. 
 
Q1 2011 results were in line with our expectations.  Software and maintenance revenues grew by 
20% (12% organically) but below the high-end of the street.  Q1 is SAP‟s smallest quarter so 
extrapolation for the full year is a little dangerous, but management reiterated their guidance for 
10% organic software and maintenance growth this year (at constant currency) and we think that 
they will manage this. 
 
Oracle 
 
Oracle reported strong Q4 numbers (May year-end): licence revenues were up 15% yoy and FCF 
was an impressive $3.7bn for the quarter, up 60%.  While software sales beat, hardware sales 
were weaker than expected, with product revenues down 6% yoy which concerned the analyst 
community. 
 
Oracle‟s hardware business is mostly the old Sun business combined with its rapid growth Exadata 
and Exalogic appliances.  The fall in revenues is entirely from the legacy Sun business, a low 
quality business as the server hardware market is moving from unix to cheaper virtualised x86 
boxes.  This business deserves to be on a low multiple, but its impact on the overall valuation of 
oracle is small: it accounts for 4% of operating profit.   
 
In contrast, Oracle‟s Exadata business is growing very rapidly: revenues were up 50% qoq and the 
growth in this business will eclipse the decline in the legacy Sun business in 2012.   
 
The stock sold off post the numbers, but this was a case of investors focusing on the wrong part of 
the business (the low quality hardware business vs the high quality Exadata and software 
businesses).  This gave us an opportunity to buy more stock cheaply on 12.5x our 2012 earnings 
estimate. 
 
Disney 
 
Disney reported earnings for its quarter ended April which were in line with our expectations but 
disappointed the market due to one-off impacts such as the effect of the Japanese earthquake on 
the Tokyo Disneyland Park and continued losses on the Playdom on line gaming company.  
However, cable network operating profits were up 15% with strong ad growth at both ABC and 
ESPN where ads were up 43% yoy.

Park pricing is rising close to double digits giving us confidence in a recovery in park earnings.  We

Viacom 
 
Viacom continues to grow advertising at double digit and affiliate fees at close to double digit.  
Operational leverage is leading to structural margin expansion.  Combined with continued 
aggressive share repurchases we have raised our free cash flow estimates for 2012 so that the 
stock trades on 10x our estimate of this number. 
 
CBS 
 
CBS‟s business continues to perform extremely strongly on all metrics.  It is much more than an 
advertising recovery play.  Structural cost reduction, multi-year uplifts in retransmission fees to 
over a billion dollars over time, increased monetization of the content library to new media 
distribution channels like Netflix, are leading to higher and more stable earnings over time.  Active 
share repurchases of around 8% pa of the market cap are further driving shareholder value.   
 
Despite a 50% increase in the shares YTD we still see the company on 10x 2012 free cash per 
share and growing this at 10-15% pa for several years.  
 
Wellpoint 
 
Medical utilisation trends remain muted this year and this has benefited the managed care industry 
as pricing has been robust. 
 
We continue to see this in reported numbers.  Wellpoint beat Q1 EPS expectations by 26% on the 
back of lower than expected medical costs and modestly better enrolment.  
 
Healthcare reform has introduced regulations which constrain absolute profitability of the managed 
care industry, particularly the MLR floors, but the consequence of this has been a significant 
reduction in competition which will lower the volatility of profits over the underwriting cycle.  Also, 
while it was painful to hear all political rhetoric against the HMOs, this has the effect of 
discouraging new capital from coming into the industry and has made high and sustained returns 
on equity more likely.   
 
With the strong year to date performance of the shares we have significantly reduced our holding.
