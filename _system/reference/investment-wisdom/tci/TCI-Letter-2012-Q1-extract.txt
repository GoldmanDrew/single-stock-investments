Portfolio Review for Q1 2012 
 
CESP 
 
In FY11, CESP delivered BRL2bn of EBITDA, BRL1.2bn of equity free cash flow (10% yield), a net 
debt/EBITDA ratio of 1.8x (vs. a company target of 2.0x) and a dividend yield of 4%.  We continue 
to forecast equity free cash flow of BRL1.4bn in FY12 (12% yield) as low priced contracts re-price 
and a rapid acceleration of dividends (forecast 9-10% yield).    
  
Recent comments by President Dilma Rousseff to the media confirm that the government wishes to 
renew the concessions.  The industry remains fully engaged with politicians to reach a satisfactory 
conclusion on terms (for example, price/duration).  We expect the São Paulo government to move 
swiftly to privatise CESP once the concessions have been renewed.  Meanwhile, CESP management 
continues to focus on efficiency improvement and the maximisation of cash flow and dividends. 
 
If the expiring concessions are renewed as we expect (and given CESP’s other above-market long 
term power contracts), then CESP cash flows would have minimal exposure to market power prices 
and are effectively guaranteed by the whole electricity supply system under which Brazil operates.  
Hence, their discount rate should be very low.  Recent auctions of new electricity transmission 
projects imply a real cost of equity of around 6% is being bid for assets with low risk cash flows. 
 
In Q1, CESP shares appreciated 11% in local currency terms as the market gained further 
conviction on concession renewal combined with the defensive attractions of an increasingly 
efficient utility with the potential for rapid dividend growth.  The Real was relatively stable this 
quarter.  We are currently hedged with forward contracts on all of the position. 
 
At the current valuation, we see a very attractive long-term real IRR of 13% and 50-70% upside to 
fair privatisation value.

Lloyds Bank bonds 
 
Lloyds bonds performed strongly in Q1.  The fund’s largest position rallied, giving a total return of 
over 20% in Q1 (including interest).  They moved from a 14% yield to maturity at year end to 
around an 11% yield to maturity.  The bank’s share price has rallied 38% year to date.  The 
announcement of the ECB’s Long Term Refinancing Operation in December to allow European 
banks to finance at 1% a wide variety of collateral was a powerful catalyst for European bank 
equity and credit to rally. 
 
90% of the fund’s position is in bonds which must pay interest as long as the core tier 1 capital is 
at least 5% under the Basle II regulatory regime.  Around 10% of the position has been held in 
“may pay” bonds which are only required to pay interest if there is a dividend paid on the common 
shares.  These latter bonds are interesting because they have no tax deductibility of coupons and 
will not have any capital treatment, so they are just expensive funding.  Lloyds confirmed that they 
would be paying interest on their “may pay” bonds and, as requested by the regulator, the bank 
issued shares to raise £170m to pay the coupons on these. 
 
In Q1 Lloyds announced 2011 full year results largely in line with our expectations.  They reported 
a core tier 1 of 10.8% (vs. 10.2% in 2010), a net interest margin of 2.2% (vs. 2.1% in 2010).  
The non-core book continued to significantly reduce to £134bn (down from £183bn in 2010), and 
we believe that it can continue to bring it down at a run-rate of around £30bn pa for the next 2 
years as a result of run off at maturity, asset sales and write-downs.   
 
The bank confirmed that it took £11bn in Long Term Repo Operation finance to be used to fund 
non-core European assets.  Lloyds Bank net exposure to Irish risk is now down to £8.4bn (gross 
exposure of £24.8bn impaired to 66%).  The main remaining component of their continental 
exposure is now around £6bn of exposure to Spain which comprises mostly mortgages.  On the 
funding side, the annual growth rate in customer deposits was 6%.  Loan to deposits is now 135% 
(down from 154% in 2010).  Impairments also continue to decrease: £9.8bn in 2011 against 
£13.3bn in 2010 but, more importantly, impairments in the core book accounted for only £2.9bn 
vs. £3.6bn in 2010.  Impairments were down across all divisions, proving the increasing quality of 
the loan book.  We believe that the core business has to a large degree normalised in asset quality 
and provisioning. 
 
Despite the challenges of limited growth in the UK economy and close to zero interest rates, we 
remain confident that the credit strength of Lloyds will continue to improve.  Lloyds will carry on 
enhancing its capital base, generating profits, and reducing its impairments charges.  The bank 
generates around £10bn of pre provision profits a year giving it strong earnings power to clean up 
and deleverage the rest of its non-core book.   
 
Japan Tobacco 
 
Japan Tobacco (JT) had a great start of the year with the stock up 29% in Japanese Yen terms in 
Q1.   
 
The company reported a strong set of numbers for the third quarter fiscal year ending March 2012.  
JT's domestic tobacco business EBITDA was up 15% yoy as volumes were better than expected 
(+47% yoy) and costs were lower than expected.  Although JT’s domestic market share is still 1% 
below its 60% guidance, market volumes are greater than anticipated and, more importantly, 
average selling prices are starting to edge up due to positive mix effects.  JT's international 
tobacco business also performed better than expected, with average selling prices up 8.3% in 
constant currency terms last quarter.  In 2011 JT’s international business grew revenue 8% and 
EBITDA 16%. 
 
The government currently holds 50% (5m shares) of JT's outstanding stock.  Legislation passed in 
November to finance reconstruction requires the government to sell up to one third of its shares in 
JT.  The government also plans to examine the possibility of selling its remaining shareholdings, 
suggesting that the full privatization of JT is a future possibility.  The government plans to sell JT 
shares to raise approximately ¥500bn (about 1.1m shares, or approximately 11% of the total

NewsCorp 
 
On our forecasts NewsCorp trades on around 10x FY13 earnings.  At the end of the current year 
the group will still have more than $10bn of gross cash on the balance sheet and we anticipate free 
cash flow of at least $4bn in 2013.  
 
At the operational level, we are entering what we believe to be an extremely strong affiliate and 
re-transmission consent re-pricing cycle over the coming 2-3 years which should support strong 
double digit earnings growth. 
 
Combined with a sharp improvement in the earnings mix away from the secularly challenged print 
and publishing segments and a continued commitment to share buybacks, we think the company 
can re-rate from the current c.10x to a 13-14x multiple.  On a midterm view, with strong earnings 
growth complemented with ongoing shareholder returns, the share offers 22-25% compounding.  
 
Disney 
 
Disney has performed well this quarter.  We had meetings with most of the media company CEOs 
or CFOs in February, including the CFO of Disney at the beginning of January.   
 
Four factors are important to consider in the current performance and what we expect over the 
coming year: 
1. Most notably, at the end of last year Disney signed a 10 year affiliate contract with Comcast.  
We believe this agreement incorporates average annual affiliate fee increases for all of Disney’s 
media networks of 7-8% pa.  This comes shortly after the agreement with the NFL covering a 
similar 10 year period which locked in around 6.5% cost increases over the contract life; 
2. The dynamics in the park division continue to improve off a very low base.  The group has been 
removing recession related discounts for the last two quarters and, more recently, headline 
pricing has also accelerated and is currently running at 3-7%; 
3. The major portion of the current capex cycle for parks is expected to peak this quarter and 
should lead to substantially higher margins and free cash flow in the coming quarters;   
4. The valuation remains low.  The group trades on 12.5x our FY13 EPS forecast, well below 
history and certainly below what we feel is achievable given the media network and park 
dynamics we expect for the coming years.

With respect to the affiliate agreement with Comcast, the deal achieves a number of things.  First, 
it is a very strong statement of support for the current industry framework by the ecosystem’s two 
largest players.  Second, based on Disney’s largest customer (Comcast) and its most powerful 
supplier (NFL), the contracts envisage 10 years of 8-9% operating profit growth.  However, we 
believe that future affiliate deals with smaller distributors are likely to be at higher prices than 
those offered to Comcast, and that total cost inflation (including other content costs and SGA) is 
likely to sum to less than the 6.5% agreed with the NFL.  For this reason we think the media 
networks business has reasonably high visibility on a decade of 10% or more operating profit 
growth.  
 
In the parks business, in our opinion, the group should see 3-4 years of solid margin expansion 
(the park operating margin was 13.5% last year compared to 18-20% in recent times and peers 
achieving well above 20%).  Cost and pricing dynamics described above will go a long way to 
narrow this gap as will the maturing of recently commissioned assets.  Fundamentally there is little 
reason why the Disney US park assets should be such a margin laggard in the sector.  We do not 
forecast a recovery to pre-recession levels but believe the current margin base is well below 
normalised earnings power, and this provides strong downside support in the more cyclical division 
of the business.  
 
On a group basis we expect five year average operating profit growth of 11% and EPS growth of 
16%.  In the short term we think these assets comfortably deserve a 15-16x earnings multiple 
compared to the current 12.5x, which points to upside over the coming 6-12 months of 25%. 
Furthermore, given the duration and visibility of the growth, the business can compound at 19-
20% midterm with limited re-rating. We see fundamental fair value at $75-80, which is 
comfortably supported by earnings power in the next 3-4 years of $4-5 per share.  
 
QR National 
 
Since the IPO in November 2010, the shares have now appreciated 49% in absolute terms. 
 
In 2012, QR expects AU$50m savings from the first voluntary redundancy programme (660 people 
have left the company).  Given the slow return to full production due to legacy mine de-watering, 
QR expects FY12 volumes to be around 15% below the original prospectus forecasts, but a return 
to normal system throughput in 2013 (around 230mt, up 18%).  Excluding estimated one-off 
restructuring costs of AU$30m, we now expect EBIT of AU$600m in 2012 rising to more than 
AU$900m in 2013, up 50%, driven by assumed normal volume throughput, cost cutting, retention 
of improved revenue quality and asset growth.   
 
The functional transformation of the business continues.  The organisational structure introduced in 
late 2011 is very similar to that adopted by the US Class 1 railroads.  We expect a second 
voluntary redundancy scheme larger than the first to be announced later this year and multi-year 
operational improvements across many aspects of the business.  QR has significant upside from 
higher asset utilisation (in part due to the pre-IPO investment) and workforce optimisation, which 
over time should help to create additional shareholder value.  QR is highly focused on improving 
service to its key customers, a pre-requisite to building a strong platform for contract re-pricing 
and growth tonnage. 
 
Management’s strategy remains centred on improving business performance and growth through 
investment.  Roughly half of QR’s operations comprise the Above Rail business (mainly rolling 
stock for transport of export coking and thermal coal mined in Queensland and New South Wales).  
The majority of current coal contracts were signed when QR was government owned at 
uneconomic returns on capital and QR has already shown the capability to sign “higher price for 
higher service” contracts. 
 
The growth prospects for QR’s Below Rail business (RAB assets) are exceptional.  QR has just 
announced the completion of the AU$1bn GAPE X50 project, on time and budget, that links the 
coal-rich Bowen Basin to Abbot Point coal terminal.  The Queensland Government has recently 
announced preferred mining consortia to expand Abbot Point from 50mt in 2012 to 350mt

Porsche SE 
 
Porsche Automobile Holding SE (Porsche) is a new position for the fund.  It is a holding company 
with two principal investments: a 50.1% stake in Porsche AG, the manufacturer of luxury sports 
cars, and a 32% economic stake (current value €18.7bn) and a 50.8% voting stake in Volkswagen 
AG.  The holding in Porsche AG is subject to a put/call option, which is exercisable for €3.9bn by 
Porsche and Volkswagen during various windows between 2012 and 2015.  The company also has 
€1.5bn in debt. 
 
In 2009 Porsche and Volkswagen signed a comprehensive agreement that was due to end in a 
merger of the two companies by the end of 2011.  This would have effectively collapsed the 
shareholding structure of the two companies, uniting them and the Porsche and Piech families into 
a single entity.  However, the merger did not happen due to the uncertainty surrounding several 
legal cases that have been brought against Porsche in the US and Germany and due to the 
investigation being conducted by the Stuttgart Public Prosecutor regarding alleged market 
manipulation by the former management of Porsche.  Porsche holds the view that these allegations 
are without merit and that the claims in the US are inadmissible.  The net value of the damages 
being sought by the plaintiffs now amounts to approximately €5-6bn.   
 
While merger talks were still ongoing, Porsche traded at a negligible discount to its net asset value.  
But when the merger was called off in September 2011 this discount widened and currently stands 
at above 30%.  We think this discount is too high.  In our opinion, the legal claims are likely to be 
settled for much less than the gross amount disclosed by the company and we also think that – on 
a 3 year view – there is a good chance that the merger with Volkswagen could once again become 
a viable way to combine the two companies.  Both of these events should result in a closing of the 
discount.  In a merger scenario the NAV is also likely to be higher as the Porsche preference 
shareholders are likely to get Volkswagen preference shares, which trade at a premium to the 
ordinary shares owned by Porsche. 
 
However, any resolution of the merger debate and the Porsche AG put/call situation is perhaps 
more than two years away so, in the meantime, Porsche should trade like a tracking stock to the 
underlying value of its holding in Volkswagen AG (VW).  This is a stock that we think is very 
attractive. 
 
VW is one of the leading automobile companies in the world.  It delivered 8.2m cars and light 
commercial vehicles in 2011, a figure that was up over 14% on 2010 and compares to the growth 
in the overall market of 4.8%.  Over the last five years, VW’s global market share has risen from 
9.6% in 2007 to 12.3% in 2011, with consistent share gains every year.  Only GM and Toyota 
produce more cars per year.  Its main brands are Volkswagen, Audi, SKODA, SEAT and Bentley 
and it also owns controlling stakes in the two truck companies, Scania and MAN.

Over 65% of VW’s revenues in its auto division come from Europe, a market that is plagued with 
overcapacity as pressure from labour unions and governments make it very hard for auto 
companies to close underutilised and loss-making factories.  In 2011, car volumes in Europe fell by 
1.5%.  As a result, European car companies such as Fiat and Peugeot struggled to make a profit.  
In contrast, VW’s car division made a 7.1% operating margin during the same year.  VW’s excess 
margins are due to a combination of its scale, its brand (which allows it to price at a premium) and 
its superior manufacturing techniques.  We think all of these factors are sustainable and that the 
gap between VW and its competitors should widen further as the company continues to invest in 
R&D, new products and more efficient production.   
 
VW also operates extremely profitable Chinese joint ventures; its share of their operating profit 
totalled €2.6bn in 2011 (up from €1.9bn in 2010).  VW’s deliveries in China were up over 17% last 
year, lifting its market share from 17% to 18% and thereby extending its leading position in the 
market.  VW plans to increase production capacity in China to around 3m vehicles a year and in 
the next 4 years the company will invest €14bn in the country, an amount entirely funded by the 
cash flow from the Chinese joint ventures.  We think that although current margins in the mid-
teens are probably unsustainable they should remain higher than the group average due to the 
Chinese desire for Western premium brands.  Chinese car penetration and especially premium car 
penetration will continue to grow structurally long term. 
 
We met with the senior management of Porsche and VW during the quarter (both companies have 
the same CEO and CFO) and we gained confidence in the long term strategy of both companies.   
 
For VW 2012 will be dominated by the need to convert its plant and equipment for use with its 
Modular Transverse Toolkit (the so-called MQB production system).  MQB will standardise many 
aspects of the company’s production platforms across many of the models in its portfolio.  The goal 
is to convert 2m units onto the new system by 2014 and 4m units by 2015.  This will result in 20% 
savings on the targeted cost base, of which half will be reinvested in new features and half will be 
kept by the company (and hence directly contribute to margin expansion).  This half is estimated 
by the company to be over €3bn.  The project will require higher near term capex and VW will also 
need to expense around €2bn this year in MQB related costs, yet despite this management still 
expect operating profits in 2012 to be flat or higher than in 2011.  This reflects significant 
resilience in the underlying business considering the continued pressures from the weak economic 
environment. 
 
On a longer term view we also expect to see accretion from the further integration of Porsche AG, 
MAN and Scania.  VW could use its huge annual free cash flow of more than €8bn to increase its 
ownership of these key assets and the expected synergies from these transactions would provide a 
substantial tailwind to earnings. 
 
We think that the VW’s earnings potential could be in excess of €25 per share by 2014/15, which 
would give us a target price of €200 and an IRR of 16% (including dividends).  This translates into 
a 3 year IRR of over 30% in Porsche, assuming the litigation is settled for €1bn and the merger 
with VW is reinstated.  Even without a merger a 25% IRR is feasible over 3 years.  The look 
through P/E on 2014 EPS of Porsche is only 3.5x.

Coal India 
 
Coal India Ltd (CIL) reported rather disappointing sales volumes for the quarter ending December 
2011 flat yoy.  A key bottleneck remains the availability of train wagons, which is now being 
addressed under pressure of the Prime Minister's Office.  More importantly, average selling prices 
were better than expected driven by higher than expected e-auction prices, up 55% yoy. 
 
We have increased the pressure on management, the Board of CIL and the Ministry of Coal to 
improve corporate governance and stop the abuse of minority shareholders in under pricing its coal 
sales.  We received strong evidence under the Right to Information (RTI) Act 2005 that the Board 
was instructed by the Ministry of Coal to reverse the price hikes which were introduced early 
January 2012.  We believe the Board of CIL is not functioning properly and failed to comply with its 
fiduciary duty to be independent.  TCI has launched a website www.coal4india.com in order to 
increase pressure on management and gain greater support for our actions to improve governance.  
 
TCI has served notice on the Government of India that it considers the Government’s recent 
conduct in relation to CIL to be in breach of various provisions of the Cypriot and UK Bilateral 
Investment Treaties with India, including India’s obligation to accord TCI’s investment in CIL fair 
and equitable treatment and not to subject TCI’s investment in CIL to measures having the effect 
equivalent to expropriation.  TCI has also announced it has instructed its Indian lawyers, Luthra & 
Luthra, to begin the process of bringing legal proceedings against CIL and its board of directors.    
  
Coal India is effectively subsidizing the (private) power sector and forced to continue to operate 
loss-making underground mines as FSA coal prices are at 70% discount to international coal 
market prices.  By not acting in the interest of the company, the Board of Coal India is effectively 
destroying a huge amount of value which affects the people of India the most given that the 
government owns 90% of the company.

Union Pacific 
 
The position in Union Pacific (UP) is currently 2% of fund’s NAV. 
 
During the quarter we went to Washington to meet with senior figures involved in the regulation of 
the railroad industry.  We met directors, lawyers and economists at the Surface Transportation 
Board (STB).  We met the President of the Association of American Railroads, advisors to 
Congressional members of the transportation committee, the CEO of the National Industrial 
Transportation League and several lawyers who represent shippers in rate cases.  We came away 
encouraged that the regulatory system should remain stable and benign for the railroads.  The 
main risk is that the STB starts to investigate whether some form of restricted reciprocal switching 
is viable in the US, a system that already exists in Canada and that has not had a major impact on 
rail profitability.  Despite this all signs point to the maintenance of the status quo for at least the 
next two years. 
 
We also had meetings with the managements of most of the North American railroads, including 
the CFO of UP.  The major concern that has arisen with all the rails recently is the acceleration in 
the deterioration of the coal business that has occurred this quarter.  The reasons for this are 
twofold and partly related.  First of all, January and February in the US were two of the warmest 
ever recorded, which led to a significant decline in heating days and hence in electricity usage in 
general.  Secondly, the increase in shale gas production has caused a huge decline in the US 
natural gas price, resulting in utilities switching generation from coal to gas.  This initially impacts 
the Eastern railroads, as they ship expensive and dirty Eastern coal which makes switching 
profitable at higher gas prices.  UP ships coal from the powder river basin, which is cheaper and 
cleaner and hence profitable for utilities to use at lower gas prices.  However, during Q1 the 
natural gas price, which had already fallen 37% in 2011, has fallen a further 28% year to date to

Outlook 
 
In our Q4 2011 letter I made the point that persistent zero and negative real rates were a powerful 
catalyst for flows into equities.  I still believe this to be the case and one aspect of this is that even 
if equities are viewed as fair value, the bond markets in the G3 would appear to have significant 
downside potential which could result in flows of money out of bonds and into equities.  This point 
is made very well in a letter which we have attached with permission by O’Shaughnessy Asset
